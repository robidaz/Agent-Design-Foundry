# Task 4 — AI Readiness Assessment Framework for the Services Practice

| | |
|---|---|
| **Profile tested** | Strategist — productization and frameworks |
| **Deliverable** | Framework + sample report + agent design |

## Context

Dynamix is building an AI services practice to help mid-market organizations adopt AI in a structured way. The first engagement run with most clients is an AI readiness assessment — a discovery conversation that produces a recommendation: where the client should start, what they need to fix first, and what ROI looks like at 12, 24, and 36 months. Today this is a manual interview-and-deliverable process that needs to scale without losing rigor.

## Problem

Design two things: (a) the readiness assessment framework itself — what we measure, why, and how we score it; and (b) an AI agent that automates the discovery intake portion of the engagement. The agent should interview a mid-market client through structured conversations or written questionnaires and produce a draft readiness report a Dynamix consultant refines for delivery.

## Scope

- Heavier on framework and judgment than on code. A prototype is welcome but not required.
- Build the framework around a typical mid-market profile: 200–2,000 employees, $50M–$1B revenue, mixed digital maturity.
- Out of scope: certification programs, partner enablement, deep technical implementation playbooks.

## Deliverables

- The readiness assessment framework: dimensions, scoring criteria, methodology, and the rationale behind your choices.
- A sample readiness report on a fictional mid-market company you create, produced using your framework.
- A design for the discovery agent: how it interviews, how it knows when it has enough information, how it hands off to the consultant. Specify the AI-vs-human split.
- A short note on productization: how this becomes a repeatable Dynamix service offering rather than a custom engagement.

## What We Are Looking For

Strategic thinking, judgment on AI vs. human roles in client-facing work, and clarity of the framework. This is the task that tests whether you can think like a partner, not just an engineer. The right answer here is opinionated; we want to see your taste.
